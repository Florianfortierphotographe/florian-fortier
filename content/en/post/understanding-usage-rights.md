Architecture photography is an art that requires significant skill and hard work to execute properly. It is used to capture the characteristics and intricate details of buildings. But when it comes to selling or sharing these images, it is important to understand the concept of licensing, specifically usage and reproduction rights. In this article, we will explore exactly what that means.

## What Are Usage Rights?

Usage rights (or distribution rights) involve allowing a person or company to use a photograph for a specific purpose, such as displaying it on a website, publishing it in a brochure, or using it on social media. 

Simply put, it is the transfer of intellectual property rights of an image from the creator to a buyer or user. These rights allow the buyer to reproduce, broadcast, or sell the image according to the conditions outlined in the licensing agreement.

## My Approach: Clear and Transparent Licensing

Personally, with every [architecture](/en/architecture/) or corporate photography service I provide, I include a transfer of usage rights for all purposes with no time limit. In short, this allows the client to distribute the images across all media, with the sole exception of reselling the photos to third parties.

It is important to note that granting usage and reproduction rights does not mean the photographer loses the intellectual property of the image. The photographer always retains the copyright, meaning they can use it for other purposes, such as licensing it to other clients, unless a different exclusive agreement is negotiated.

## The Difference with Reproduction Rights

Reproduction rights are similar to usage rights, but they specifically allow a person or company to reproduce the photograph for a specific use, such as printing or distributing the photograph to third parties. Just like usage rights, reproduction rights can be granted for single or multiple uses.

It is also worth highlighting that when commissioning a photo shoot, it can be highly beneficial to involve all parties or companies who might benefit from the project's photos. This way, during the quoting process, the cost of the shoot can be split among the parties, with only the cost of the respective usage rights added for each individual entity.

## In Conclusion

The transfer of usage and reproduction rights is an essential part of the agreement between a photographer and their clients. It allows clients to freely promote their business without fear of copyright infringement, while allowing the photographer to protect their work and offer a high-quality, professional service.
